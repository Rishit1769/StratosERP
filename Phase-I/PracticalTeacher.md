🧪 1. Lab Execution & Practical Operations

The Lab / Practical Instructor is responsible for conducting and managing all practical sessions in a structured, batch-wise environment.

Lab Session Execution: Conduct assigned lab sessions based on timetable or manual assignment, ensuring proper batch handling.
Experiment Management: Execute and track individual experiments within each subject, maintaining consistency across batches.
Batch Handling: Manage practical sessions for specific student batches (A1, A2, etc.), ensuring controlled and scalable lab operations.
Session Ownership: Each lab session is owned by a single assigned faculty, ensuring accountability and preventing data conflicts.
📊 2. Practical Evaluation & Marking

The instructor evaluates students based on detailed, experiment-level performance metrics rather than aggregated marks.

Experiment-Level Marking: Assign marks for:
Viva
Execution / Performance
Journal / File work
Granular Assessment: Maintain per-experiment records instead of only final practical scores.
Remarks & Feedback: Provide qualitative feedback for each student to highlight strengths and weaknesses.
Session-Based Evaluation: Marks are tied to specific lab sessions, ensuring traceability and auditability.
📅 3. Lab Attendance & Submission Tracking

The system ensures accurate tracking of student participation and work completion in practical environments.

Lab Attendance: Record attendance per session with strict one-entry-per-student rules.
Submission Monitoring: Track experiment file submissions, including:
Submitted
Late
Missing
Participation Insights: Identify irregular or absent students based on lab attendance patterns.
Batch-Level Visibility: Attendance and submission tracking is maintained per batch for operational clarity.
🔐 4. Access Control & Session Integrity

The module enforces strict assignment-based access control to maintain data integrity and prevent conflicts.

Assignment-Based Access: Only the faculty assigned to a lab session can:
Mark attendance
Enter marks
Add remarks
Session Locking: Once a session is marked as completed and locked:
No further edits are allowed
Audit Trail: All updates are tracked with:
Faculty ID
Timestamp
No Multi-Faculty Editing: Prevents overwriting and ensures a single source of truth.
🔄 5. Faculty Flexibility & Assignment Model

The system supports flexible faculty involvement without compromising control.

Multi-Role Compatibility: Any faculty (Subject Teacher, Class Incharge, TG, or Lab Instructor) can conduct practicals if assigned.
Substitution Handling: Supports reassignment in case of planned or emergency leave scenarios.
Priority-Based Allocation:
Primary: Lab Instructor
Secondary: Subject Teacher
Backup: Class Incharge
Last Resort: Teacher Guardian
Dynamic Assignment: Faculty responsibility is determined per session, not permanently fixed by role.
🔗 6. Integration with Core ERP Systems

The Lab module is tightly integrated with existing ERP components to ensure consistency and scalability.

Timetable Engine Integration: Lab sessions can be auto-generated from timetable slots.
Student Dashboard Integration: Practical marks, attendance, and feedback are visible to students in real time.
HOD Oversight: Department heads can monitor lab performance trends and faculty efficiency.
AI Layer (Gemini):
Generate performance insights
Detect weak students
Flag attendance issues
🔑 7. Operational Context

The Lab / Practical Instructor operates within a controlled academic framework aligned with institutional rules.

Assignment-Driven Execution: Authority is defined per session rather than by static role hierarchy.
Branch & Subject Scope: Access is limited to assigned subjects and batches within the department.
Independent Practical System: The lab module operates separately from theory systems such as lecture logs and final marks.
Semester Alignment: All lab activities follow the global semester configuration set by the Admin.
